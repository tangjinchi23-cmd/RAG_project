1. Principles for matching skin tone with clothing colors
   Cool fair skin: suits both cool and warm tones; bright colors (such as royal blue, true red, mint green) make the skin look brighter and more radiant; dark colors (such as black, dark gray) boost presence, and avoid looking overly pale.
   Yellow / warm-yellow skin: prioritize warm tones (such as caramel, ginger yellow, bean-paste red), and avoid cool fluorescent colors (such as fluorescent green, cool pink) which easily make the skin look dull; light beige and oatmeal soften the complexion and improve the look.
   Dark skin: suits high-saturation bright colors (such as bright yellow, orange, lake blue) to highlight healthy skin; avoid dull earthy yellow and grayish brown, which make the skin look dull and lifeless.

2. Choosing clothing colors by occasion
   Daily commute: mainly basic colors (black, white, gray, beige, navy), simple and elegant; can be accented with low-saturation bright colors (such as hazy blue, light purple) to add vitality.
   Formal occasions (business meetings / interviews): prefer dark tones (black, navy, dark gray), steady and professional; avoid large areas of bright color and flashy patterns, keep it clean and appropriate.
   Casual occasions (shopping / outings): choose high-saturation colors or contrasting combinations (such as yellow + white, blue + white), fresh and lively; basic patterns like stripes and plaid also suit casual settings.
   Banquets / parties: choose bright colors (true red, wine red, royal blue) or metallic colors (gold, silver) to highlight your presence; avoid overly plain colors that reduce your presence.

3. Techniques for flattering body shapes with clothing colors
   Slimming look: prefer dark tones (black, dark gray, navy) to visually contract the figure; avoid large areas of bright color and horizontal stripes, which easily look bulky.
   Height-enhancing look: light on top and dark on the bottom (such as a light pink top + black pants) creates visual contrast and lengthens the proportions; monochrome combinations (all beige, all black) make the lines look smooth.
   Fuller figure: avoid tight + bright color combinations, choose matte-fabric dark clothing, paired with small areas of bright accessories (such as scarves, necklaces) to brighten the look.
   Petite figure: suits light and warm tones (such as white, beige, light yellow), visually fuller; avoid overly loose dark clothing, which easily looks thin.

4. Choosing clothing colors by season
   Spring: suits fresh and soft colors (such as cherry-blossom pink, mint green, light blue), matching the atmosphere of everything coming back to life.
   Summer: suits cool tones and light colors (such as white, sky blue, light green), cool and refreshing; avoid dark tones, which absorb heat and look heavy.
   Autumn: suits warm tones and earthy colors (such as caramel, khaki, brown), echoing the feel of falling leaves and harvest.
   Winter: suits dark tones and high-saturation bright colors (such as black, wine red, dark green), warm and commanding; a white down jacket is also a classic winter choice, clean and crisp.

5. Color-matching taboos
   Avoid large areas of high-saturation color clashes (such as red + green, blue + orange), which easily look messy and harsh; use neutral colors (black, white, gray) as a transition.
   Yellow skin should avoid cool pink and purple, which easily look sallow; dark skin should avoid fluorescent colors, which easily look dull.
   For formal occasions avoid combining more than three colors, keep it clean and coordinated; for casual occasions you can add color layers, but keep a clear primary and secondary structure.

6. Techniques for matching accessory colors
   Same-tone accessories: matching the clothing color (such as a black top + black bag), simple and refined, suitable for commuting.
   Contrasting accessories: contrasting with the clothing color (such as a white dress + red necklace), brightening the look, suitable for casual wear and parties.
   Neutral accessories: black, white, gray, and metallic accessories go with everything and can match clothing of any color; they are the "safe bet" of matching.
